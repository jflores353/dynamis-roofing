Residential Roof System

  1. Asphalt Shingle

    Asphalt shingle are one of the most common roofing systems in the United States. They are the most budget friendly roofing systems you can get for a full roof replacement. They come in many different types of colors, and they can always be replaced in 10 – 20 years. Shingles being the most popular roof systems out here in Central Texas Austin, you can count on us to replace your shingle roof.

    2. Pros to a shingle roof:

    • Most affordable roof system
    • Easy to remove and replace
    • Low maintenance
    • Available in a wide range of colors and styles

    Cons to a Shingle Roof:

    • Less resistance to algae
    • Replacing every 10-15 year
    • Less likely to hold up to hail or high winds

  2. Tile Roof

    One of the most beautiful roof systems there is to install, this roof system will last you 100 years when properly maintenance. They are known for being durable and heavy but that is expected when they will last you 100 years. They are an expenses system to install but it’s the beats having to replace your roof every 10- 15 years. They can withstand hail and wind a lot stronger than your asphalt shingle system.

    3. Pros of Tile Roof:

    • Long lasting
    • Energy efficient
    • Low maintenance
    • Good for resale value

    Cons of Tile Roof:

    • Weighs more than your normal roof system
    • Suffers damage from fallen trees or other impacts
    • Requires exper installation, maintenance, and repairs

  3. Metal

    Metal roofs are becoming popular with the new Morden design in the real estate market. More people are looking to have that black and white look on their house. With this increase in popularity comes an abundant amount of options. A metal roof can add style and flair to your Central Texas Home. Austin Texas is seeing more metal roofs being put up because of the longevity it comes with expecting to last you 40- 70 years.

    4. Pros of Metal Roof:

    • Resistance to corrosion and cracking
    • Extremely long lifespan
    • Wind resistant roofing material
    • Streamlined aesthetic

    Cons of Metal Roof:

    • High upfront costs compared to other roofing materials
    • Noise during rain or hailstorms
    • Risk of denting, fading, staining, or rusting
    • Difficulty of modification and repairs
    • Slippery and hot surface
